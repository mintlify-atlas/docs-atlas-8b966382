Viewfinder cameras are the heart of Vista's broadcast system, allowing you to capture live footage from anywhere in your world and display it on TVs.

## Placing a Viewfinder

The viewfinder is a block that acts as a camera, capturing what it "sees" and broadcasting it to connected TVs.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Craft a Viewfinder">
    Craft a viewfinder using the crafting recipe (check your recipe book in-game).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Your Location">
    Place the viewfinder block in the location where you want to capture footage. Consider:
    - **Visibility**: What you want the camera to see
    - **Safety**: Protected areas where it won't be destroyed
    - **Accessibility**: Easy to reach for adjustments
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Position the Block">
    The viewfinder's orientation matters. The direction you face when placing it determines its initial viewing angle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adjusting Camera Angles

Once placed, you can control the viewfinder's viewing angle to capture exactly what you want.

### Controlling Pitch and Yaw

Right-click the viewfinder to enter control mode. While controlling:

- **Yaw (Horizontal Rotation)**: Move your mouse left and right to pan the camera
- **Pitch (Vertical Rotation)**: Move your mouse up and down to tilt the camera
- **Zoom**: The viewfinder supports up to **44x zoom** for detailed shots

<Note>
  The viewfinder uses smooth interpolation for camera movement, creating professional-looking footage without jarring transitions.
</Note>

### Camera Constraints

The viewfinder has built-in rotation constraints based on how it's mounted:

- Pitch is clamped within configured min/max values
- Yaw is restricted to prevent unnatural rotation
- These constraints ensure realistic camera behavior

### Locking the Camera

You can lock a viewfinder's position to prevent accidental changes:

1. Adjust the camera to your desired angle
2. Set the lock flag (implementation varies by integration)
3. Locked cameras will maintain their exact position

<Warning>
  Only players who can access the viewfinder block can control it. Protect your cameras in claimed or protected areas.
</Warning>

## Advanced Camera Features

### Field of View (FOV)

The viewfinder dynamically adjusts its FOV based on zoom level:

- **Base FOV**: 70 degrees (matches Minecraft's default)
- **Zoom calculation**: FOV decreases as zoom increases
- **Maximum zoom**: Creates a spyglass-like effect at 1/5 normal FOV

### Near Plane Rendering

The camera uses a near plane value of `0.05` blocks, allowing it to capture objects very close to the lens without clipping.

### Adding Filters

You can modify the viewfinder's output by inserting items:

- **Stained Glass Panes**: Add color tinting to the camera feed
- **Supplementaries Shaders** (if installed): Apply special visual effects

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sneak + Right-Click">
    Hold shift and right-click the viewfinder with an empty hand or compatible item.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Insert Filter">
    Place a stained glass pane or shader item into the viewfinder.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View Effect">
    The camera feed will now display with the applied filter.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices

### Strategic Camera Placement

1. **Mob Farms**: Monitor spawning rates and collection systems
2. **Base Entrances**: Security cameras for your front door
3. **Redstone Contraptions**: Watch complex machinery in action
4. **Scenic Views**: Capture beautiful landscapes or builds
5. **Event Spaces**: Cover arenas, theaters, or gathering areas

### Multiple Camera Setups

Each viewfinder has a unique UUID (stored in NBT data at `ViewFinderBlockEntity.java:43`) that identifies it in the broadcast system. You can set up as many cameras as you need:

- Create different cameras for different areas
- Switch between feeds by changing cassettes in your TV
- Build a security room with multiple TVs showing different locations

### Performance Considerations

Viewfinders are relatively lightweight:

- The block entity stores only position data (yaw, pitch, zoom)
- Video processing happens on the client side
- Server performance impact is minimal

<Note>
  Viewfinders tick only to smoothly interpolate rotation values (see `ViewFinderBlockEntity.java:73`), making them efficient for always-on camera systems.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

### Camera Won't Move

- Check if the camera is locked
- Ensure you have permission to interact with the block
- Verify you're in control mode (right-clicked the viewfinder)

### Feed Not Updating

- Ensure the viewfinder is in a loaded chunk
- Check that the TV is properly linked (see [Linking Feeds](/guides/linking-feeds))
- Verify the dimension matches (cameras and TVs must be in the same dimension)

### Can't Insert Filters

- Hold shift while right-clicking
- Ensure you're using a compatible item (stained glass panes)
- Remove existing filters before inserting new ones
