# Quickstart Guide

Get started with Vista by building your first television setup, playing a cassette, and setting up a live camera feed.

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- Vista mod installed (see [Installation](/installation))
- A Minecraft world with creative or survival mode
- Basic crafting materials

## Step 1: Craft a Television

First, let's craft a television block.

**Recipe**: Shaped crafting

```
[Tinted Glass] [Glass Pane  ] [         ]
[Tinted Glass] [Diamond     ] [Redstone ]
[Tinted Glass] [Glass Pane  ] [         ]
```

**Materials needed**:
- 3x Tinted Glass
- 2x Any Planks
- 1x Diamond
- 1x Redstone

<Note>
The recipe uses a diamond in the center, representing the screen technology.
</Note>

## Step 2: Place Your First TV

<Steps>
  <Step title="Place the television">
    Right-click to place the television block. It will face away from you by default.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Power the TV">
    Apply a redstone signal to the TV block. You can use:
    - A lever on the block
    - Redstone dust next to it
    - Any redstone power source
    
    The TV will light up when powered!
  </Step>

  <Step title="Expand to multi-block (optional)">
    Place more TV blocks adjacent to your first one to create a larger screen:
    - Place blocks to the right, left, above, or below
    - TVs automatically connect into a single large display
    - Maximum size: 8x8 blocks (configurable)
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Hold sneak (shift) while placing TVs if you want to prevent them from automatically connecting.
</Tip>

## Step 3: Get a Cassette Tape

There are several ways to obtain cassette tapes:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Loot Chests">
    Cassette tapes have a 3% chance to appear in treasure loot chests. Explore the world and open chests to find them!
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Creeper Drops">
    When a Creeper is killed by a Pillager, it has a chance to drop a cassette tape. Set up a mob farm or find a Pillager outpost!
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Custom Naming">
    If you're a supporter, you can create a cassette and rename it on an anvil to match a supporter cassette name. The cassette will transform into the supporter version!
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Creative Mode">
    In creative mode, find cassette tapes in the Tools and Utilities creative tab. Each cassette shows a different animation!
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Step 4: Play a Cassette

<Steps>
  <Step  title="Insert the cassette">
    Right-click on the powered TV with a cassette tape in your hand. You'll hear an insertion sound.
  </Step>

  <Step  title="Watch the video">
    The cassette will start playing automatically. Each cassette has a unique looping animation!
  </Step>

  <Step  title="Pause/unpause">
    Right-click the TV with an empty hand to pause or unpause playback.
  </Step>

  <Step  title="Eject the cassette">
    Right-click with an empty hand while sneaking to eject the cassette.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 5: Set Up a Live Camera Feed

Now let's create a surveillance system with a viewfinder camera.

### Craft a Viewfinder

**Recipe**: Shaped crafting

```
[Iron Ingot] [Iron Ingot] [Iron Ingot]
[Iron Ingot] [Spyglass  ] [Iron Ingot]
[Planks    ] [Planks    ] [Planks    ]
```

**Materials needed**:
- 5x Iron Ingots
- 1x Spyglass
- 3x Any Planks

### Place the Viewfinder

<Steps>
  <Step  title="Place the camera">
    Right-click to place the viewfinder. It will automatically orient based on where you're looking.
  </Step>

  <Step  title="Adjust the angle">
    The viewfinder will point in the direction you were facing when you placed it. You can fine-tune it later.
  </Step>

  <Step  title="Use the camera (optional)">
    Right-click the viewfinder with an empty hand to enter camera control mode. You can:
    - Look around to adjust pitch and yaw
    - Scroll to zoom in/out (1x to 44x)
    - Press your dismount key to exit
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Create a Hollow Cassette

To link the viewfinder to a TV, you need a hollow cassette:

**Recipe**: Shapeless crafting

```
Cassette + Echo Shard = Hollow Cassette
```

### Link the Feed

<Steps>
  <Step  title="Link to viewfinder">
    Right-click the viewfinder with the hollow cassette. The cassette will now be linked to this camera's location.
    
    The cassette tooltip will show: "Linked to [X, Y, Z]\"
  </Step>

  <Step  title="Insert into TV">
    Take the linked hollow cassette to your TV and right-click to insert it.
  </Step>

  <Step  title="Watch the live feed">
    The TV will now display a live feed from the viewfinder's perspective! The camera updates in real-time based on its orientation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Live feeds work across dimensions! You can place a camera in the Nether and watch it from the Overworld.
</Note>

## Tips & Tricks

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion  title="How do I make a bigger TV?">
    Simply place more TV blocks adjacent to each other. They automatically form a connected display up to 8x8 blocks (configurable in the config).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion  title="Can I wear a TV as a helmet?">
    Yes! TVs can be equipped in the helmet slot. Right-click while holding a TV to wear it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion  title="How do I use redstone comparators with TVs?">
    TVs emit a comparator signal when they have a cassette inserted. Different cassettes emit different signal strengths (1-15), allowing for redstone contraptions based on which cassette is playing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion  title="Can multiple TVs show the same camera feed?">
    Absolutely! Create multiple hollow cassettes linked to the same viewfinder location, and insert them into different TVs. All TVs will show the same live feed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion  title="What about performance with multiple cameras?">
    Vista uses an adaptive update system. Cameras farther away or on smaller screens update less frequently to maintain good performance.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card  title="TV won't turn on" icon="exclamation-triangle">
    - Make sure the TV has a redstone signal
    - Check that a cassette is inserted
    - Verify the cassette is not paused
  </Card>

  <Card  title="Live feed shows black screen" icon="circle-exclamation">
    - Verify the viewfinder exists at the linked location
    - Check that the chunks are loaded
    - Make sure you're within render distance
  </Card>

  <Card  title="Low frame rate" icon="gauge-simple-low">
    - Move closer to the TV
    - Use smaller TV screens
    - Disable NTSC effects in config
    - Reduce update distance in config
  </Card>

  <Card  title="Cassette won't insert" icon="ban">
    - Make sure the TV is powered
    - Verify you're clicking the master block (bottom-left of multi-block TVs)
    - Check that no cassette is already inserted
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
